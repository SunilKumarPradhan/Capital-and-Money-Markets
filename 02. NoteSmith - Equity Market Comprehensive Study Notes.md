## Stocks and Shares - The Foundation

### 🏢 What is a Share?

> **Definition**: A share (also called "equity" or "stock") represents a unit of ownership in a company. When you buy a share, you become a part-owner of that company with proportional claims on its assets and earnings.

### Understanding Ownership

When you own shares, you have:

1. **Claim on Assets**: If the company were liquidated, you'd have a proportional claim on what remains after debts are paid
2. **Claim on Earnings**: You're entitled to a portion of the company's profits (either as dividends or reinvested for growth)
3. **Voting Rights**: (For common shares) You can vote on major corporate decisions

### 📊 Real-World Example

```python
# Simple Share Ownership Calculator

class ShareOwnership:
    def __init__(self, company_name, total_shares, share_price):
        self.company_name = company_name
        self.total_shares = total_shares
        self.share_price = share_price
        self.market_cap = total_shares * share_price
    
    def calculate_ownership(self, shares_owned):
        """
        Calculate ownership percentage and value
        
        Args:
            shares_owned: Number of shares you own
        
        Returns:
            Dictionary with ownership details
        """
        ownership_percentage = (shares_owned / self.total_shares) * 100
        investment_value = shares_owned * self.share_price
        
        return {
            'shares_owned': shares_owned,
            'ownership_percentage': round(ownership_percentage, 4),
            'investment_value': investment_value,
            'company_market_cap': self.market_cap
        }
    
    def calculate_earnings_claim(self, shares_owned, company_profit):
        """
        Calculate your claim on company earnings
        
        Args:
            shares_owned: Number of shares you own
            company_profit: Total company profit
        
        Returns:
            Your proportional claim on earnings
        """
        ownership_percentage = shares_owned / self.total_shares
        earnings_claim = company_profit * ownership_percentage
        
        return round(earnings_claim, 2)

# Example Usage
apple = ShareOwnership(
    company_name="Apple Inc.",
    total_shares=16_000_000_000,  # 16 billion shares outstanding
    share_price=175.00  # Current share price
)

# You buy 100 shares
my_investment = apple.calculate_ownership(shares_owned=100)
print(f"Company: {apple.company_name}")
print(f"Shares Owned: {my_investment['shares_owned']}")
print(f"Ownership: {my_investment['ownership_percentage']}%")
print(f"Investment Value: ${my_investment['investment_value']:,.2f}")
print(f"Company Market Cap: ${my_investment['company_market_cap']:,.0f}")

# If Apple makes $100 billion in profit
annual_profit = 100_000_000_000
my_earnings_claim = apple.calculate_earnings_claim(100, annual_profit)
print(f"\nYour claim on earnings: ${my_earnings_claim:,.2f}")
```

**Output Explanation**:
- **Shares Owned**: The number of shares you purchased
- **Ownership Percentage**: Your proportional ownership (typically very small for large companies)
- **Investment Value**: Current market value of your holdings
- **Earnings Claim**: Your proportional share of company profits

### Why Companies Issue Shares

Companies issue shares to:
1. **Raise Capital**: Get money for expansion without taking on debt
2. **Provide Liquidity**: Allow early investors and employees to cash out
3. **Currency for Acquisitions**: Use shares to buy other companies
4. **Attract Talent**: Offer stock options to employees

## Raising Capital - How Companies Fund Growth

### 💰 Two Primary Methods

Companies need capital to operate, expand, and innovate. They have two fundamental options:

### 1. Equity Financing

**What it is**: Selling ownership stakes (shares) in the company

**How it works**:
```python
class EquityFinancing:
    def __init__(self, company_name, current_shares, current_valuation):
        self.company_name = company_name
        self.current_shares = current_shares
        self.current_valuation = current_valuation
        self.price_per_share = current_valuation / current_shares
    
    def raise_capital(self, capital_needed):
        """
        Calculate dilution from issuing new shares
        
        Args:
            capital_needed: Amount of money to raise
        
        Returns:
            Details about the equity raise
        """
        # Calculate new shares to issue
        new_shares = capital_needed / self.price_per_share
        total_shares_after = self.current_shares + new_shares
        
        # Calculate dilution for existing shareholders
        dilution_percentage = (new_shares / total_shares_after) * 100
        
        # New ownership percentages
        existing_ownership = (self.current_shares / total_shares_after) * 100
        new_investor_ownership = (new_shares / total_shares_after) * 100
        
        return {
            'capital_raised': capital_needed,
            'new_shares_issued': int(new_shares),
            'total_shares_after': int(total_shares_after),
            'dilution_percentage': round(dilution_percentage, 2),
            'existing_shareholder_ownership': round(existing_ownership, 2),
            'new_investor_ownership': round(new_investor_ownership, 2)
        }

# Example: Startup raising Series A funding
startup = EquityFinancing(
    company_name="TechStartup Inc.",
    current_shares=10_000_000,  # 10 million shares
    current_valuation=50_000_000  # $50 million valuation
)

# Raising $10 million
series_a = startup.raise_capital(capital_needed=10_000_000)

print(f"Capital Raised: ${series_a['capital_raised']:,}")
print(f"New Shares Issued: {series_a['new_shares_issued']:,}")
print(f"Total Shares After: {series_a['total_shares_after']:,}")
print(f"Dilution: {series_a['dilution_percentage']}%")
print(f"Existing Shareholders Now Own: {series_a['existing_shareholder_ownership']}%")
print(f"New Investors Own: {series_a['new_investor_ownership']}%")
```

**Advantages of Equity Financing**:
- ✅ No repayment obligation
- ✅ No interest payments
- ✅ Investors share the risk
- ✅ Brings expertise and networks (especially with VC investors)

**Disadvantages**:
- ❌ Ownership dilution
- ❌ Loss of control
- ❌ Profit sharing with more people
- ❌ Pressure from shareholders

### 2. Debt Financing

**What it is**: Borrowing money that must be repaid with interest

**How it works**:
```python
class DebtFinancing:
    def __init__(self, company_name, loan_amount, interest_rate, term_years):
        self.company_name = company_name
        self.loan_amount = loan_amount
        self.interest_rate = interest_rate  # Annual rate as decimal (e.g., 0.08 for 8%)
        self.term_years = term_years
    
    def calculate_payments(self):
        """
        Calculate loan payments and total cost
        
        Returns:
            Payment schedule details
        """
        # Monthly interest rate
        monthly_rate = self.interest_rate / 12
        num_payments = self.term_years * 12
        
        # Monthly payment formula (amortization)
        if monthly_rate > 0:
            monthly_payment = self.loan_amount * (
                monthly_rate * (1 + monthly_rate)**num_payments
            ) / ((1 + monthly_rate)**num_payments - 1)
        else:
            monthly_payment = self.loan_amount / num_payments
        
        total_paid = monthly_payment * num_payments
        total_interest = total_paid - self.loan_amount
        
        return {
            'loan_amount': self.loan_amount,
            'monthly_payment': round(monthly_payment, 2),
            'total_payments': num_payments,
            'total_amount_paid': round(total_paid, 2),
            'total_interest_paid': round(total_interest, 2),
            'interest_rate_annual': self.interest_rate * 100
        }
    
    def assess_startup_viability(self, monthly_revenue, monthly_expenses):
        """
        Check if a startup can afford debt financing
        
        Args:
            monthly_revenue: Current monthly revenue
            monthly_expenses: Current monthly operating expenses
        
        Returns:
            Viability assessment
        """
        payment_details = self.calculate_payments()
        monthly_payment = payment_details['monthly_payment']
        
        # Calculate debt service coverage ratio (DSCR)
        # DSCR = Net Operating Income / Debt Service
        net_income = monthly_revenue - monthly_expenses
        
        if monthly_payment > 0:
            dscr = net_income / monthly_payment
        else:
            dscr = 0
        
        # Banks typically want DSCR > 1.25
        can_afford = dscr >= 1.25
        
        return {
            'monthly_payment': monthly_payment,
            'monthly_net_income': net_income,
            'debt_service_coverage_ratio': round(dscr, 2),
            'can_afford': can_afford,
            'recommendation': 'Approved' if can_afford else 'Too Risky - Consider Equity'
        }

# Example: Startup considering a loan
startup_loan = DebtFinancing(
    company_name="TechStartup Inc.",
    loan_amount=500_000,  # $500k loan
    interest_rate=0.08,   # 8% annual interest
    term_years=5
)

payment_info = startup_loan.calculate_payments()
print("=== Loan Details ===")
print(f"Loan Amount: ${payment_info['loan_amount']:,}")
print(f"Monthly Payment: ${payment_info['monthly_payment']:,}")
print(f"Total Interest: ${payment_info['total_interest_paid']:,}")
print(f"Total Paid: ${payment_info['total_amount_paid']:,}")

# Check if startup can afford it
viability = startup_loan.assess_startup_viability(
    monthly_revenue=50_000,
    monthly_expenses=40_000
)

print("\n=== Viability Assessment ===")
print(f"Monthly Net Income: ${viability['monthly_net_income']:,}")
print(f"Monthly Debt Payment: ${viability['monthly_payment']:,}")
print(f"DSCR: {viability['debt_service_coverage_ratio']}")
print(f"Recommendation: {viability['recommendation']}")
```

**Advantages of Debt Financing**:
- ✅ No ownership dilution
- ✅ Tax-deductible interest payments
- ✅ Predictable payment schedule
- ✅ Relationship ends after repayment

**Disadvantages**:
- ❌ Must repay regardless of business performance
- ❌ Interest payments reduce cash flow
- ❌ Requires collateral (difficult for startups)
- ❌ Risk of bankruptcy if unable to repay

### 🎯 Why Startups Prefer Equity

**The Startup Dilemma**:

1. **Lack of Collateral**: Startups often have few physical assets to pledge
2. **Uncertain Revenue**: Banks want predictable cash flow for repayment
3. **High Risk**: Traditional lenders avoid high-risk ventures
4. **Cash Flow Pressure**: Interest payments strain limited resources
